# editorial guidelines

## Required structure
1. Intent-aligned introduction (state reader problem and promised outcome)
2. Scannable body with descriptive H2s/H3s
3. At least one concrete example per major section
4. Actionable close with next-step CTA

## Writing rules
- One idea per paragraph where possible
- Prefer active voice
- Define niche terms briefly before using acronyms
- If a claim could be debated, label it as recommendation/opinion unless sourced

## Information gain checklist
Each article should add at least two of:
- A decision framework
- A practical template/checklist
- A step-by-step implementation path
- A nuanced tradeoff explanation

## Editorial QA before publish
- Does each section directly support the search intent?
- Are headings specific enough to stand alone in a table of contents?
- Are examples realistic for small teams, not enterprise-only?
- Is the CTA aligned to the reader's stage (awareness vs decision)?
