# Research Brief: Content Operations Workflow for Small SaaS Teams

- objective: Create an intent-matched, differentiation-first article brief that helps lean SaaS teams design a repeatable content ops workflow and naturally introduces LanternLoop Studio as a practical next step.
- audience: Head of Content, founder-operator, and agency strategist supporting small B2B SaaS teams.
- target keyword: content operations workflow for small SaaS teams
- search intent: Primarily informational with commercial investigation undertone (reader wants a practical system they can implement, then compare tooling/process options).

## audience pains
- Publishing depends on one "hero" editor, creating bottlenecks.
- SMEs provide insights, but drafts lose specificity during handoff.
- Content calendars exist, yet execution quality varies week to week.
- Teams struggle to connect SEO planning to conversion outcomes.

## key questions to answer
1. What is the minimum viable content ops workflow for a small SaaS team?
2. Which roles are required vs optional when headcount is limited?
3. How should teams move from topic idea to brief to publish-ready draft?
4. What QA gates prevent generic, low-converting articles?
5. When should a team standardize with software versus docs/checklists?

## likely competitor content patterns
- High-level process diagrams with little implementation detail.
- Enterprise-oriented frameworks that assume large teams and specialist roles.
- Generic AI content advice that ignores factual QA and editorial voice control.

## differentiation angle
"Operator-grade workflow" framing: show a concrete, lean-team workflow with explicit handoff rules, quality gates, and decision criteria for when to add tooling.

## recommended article angle
**Working angle:** "The 7-step content operations workflow small SaaS teams can run weekly (without hiring a full editorial department)."

## outline
1. Why most small-team content ops break after strategy
2. The 7-step weekly workflow
   - H3: Step 1 — Capture source insights from SMEs
   - H3: Step 2 — Map one keyword to one intent promise
   - H3: Step 3 — Build a brief that reduces rewrite risk
   - H3: Step 4 — Draft for clarity before optimization
   - H3: Step 5 — Run editorial + SEO QA gates
   - H3: Step 6 — Publish with conversion path checks
   - H3: Step 7 — Refresh winners and retire underperformers
3. Lean role design: who owns what when the team is tiny
4. Common failure modes and fixes
5. 30-day implementation checklist
6. Next-step CTA (demo/trial/checklist)

## conversion opportunities
- Mid-article CTA: download "Weekly Content Ops QA Checklist"
- BOFU CTA: book a LanternLoop Studio demo for workflow setup
- Lower-friction CTA: start a 14-day trial and run one full brief→draft cycle
