# Research Brief: Answer Engine Optimization for B2B SaaS

## objective
Produce an actionable research brief that can be used to draft an intent-matched article on how B2B SaaS marketing teams should adapt SEO programs for AI answer engines (ChatGPT search, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Gemini, Claude).

## audience
Heads of content, demand-gen leads, and SEO managers at B2B SaaS companies (10–500 employees) who already run an SEO program and are seeing organic traffic flatten or decline as AI answer engines absorb top-of-funnel queries.

## target keyword
answer engine optimization for B2B SaaS

## search intent
Mixed informational + commercial investigation. Reader knows AI search is changing referral patterns and wants (1) a working definition that distinguishes AEO from classic SEO, (2) concrete tactics they can execute this quarter, and (3) signals to evaluate vendors/tooling. They are skeptical of generic "AEO" think-pieces and want B2B SaaS specifics — not e-commerce or local-business advice.

## outline
1. What changed: traffic flattening even as rankings hold, and why
2. AEO vs. SEO vs. GEO — defining the terms without hand-waving
3. The B2B SaaS twist: longer cycles, smaller TAM, citations matter more than clicks
4. A 30/60/90 AEO playbook for an existing SaaS content program
5. Measurement: what to track when "sessions" stop telling the truth
6. Tooling and vendor evaluation criteria
7. Common mistakes and how to avoid them
8. Recommended next action

### audience

### pains
- "My rankings are fine but sessions are down 20–40% YoY." Reader can't separate AI cannibalization from algorithm volatility.
- No agreed measurement framework — GA4 session counts no longer reflect brand reach.
- Internal pressure to "do AEO" with no shared definition of what that means.
- Existing content was written for crawlers, not for being quoted verbatim.
- Confusion about whether to block, allow, or selectively allow LLM crawlers.
- Schema/structured-data fatigue: "we already do schema, isn't that enough?"
- Vendor noise: every SEO tool now claims "AI search tracking" with wildly different methodologies.

### questions
- What is the actual mechanism — are LLMs crawling my site, or training on cached copies, or retrieving via search APIs?
- How do I tell if my content is being cited in AI answers, and at what rate vs. competitors?
- What edits to existing articles produce the highest lift in citation rate?
- Do I need separate pages for AEO, or can I retrofit my existing library?
- Should I block GPTBot/ClaudeBot/PerplexityBot? What's the tradeoff?
- How does AEO interact with classic technical SEO — do I still care about Core Web Vitals?
- What's the right KPI to put on a slide for the CMO?
- How do my comparison/alternatives pages need to change to win in AI answers?
- What's the role of third-party mentions (G2, Reddit, podcasts) vs. owned content?
- How should I budget AEO work against classic SEO work in the next planning cycle?

### outline
1. **The traffic-flat-but-rankings-fine problem** — sized with a credible scenario, why it's happening (AI Overviews + zero-click + retrieval-augmented answers), why B2B SaaS feels it harder than e-commerce.
2. **Definitions that survive a CMO meeting** — AEO, GEO, LLM SEO, classic SEO; what's the same, what's actually new.
3. **What B2B SaaS changes about the playbook** — small TAM, account-based buying, citation share matters more than session share, brand mentions in research answers map directly to pipeline.
4. **The 30/60/90 retrofit playbook**:
   - 30 days: crawler access audit, llms.txt, citation baseline (Ahrefs Brand Radar / Profound / Peec / manual prompts), top-20 page audit for AEO-friendliness.
   - 60 days: rewrite top-of-funnel pages for "quotable" passages, structured comparison tables, FAQ sections written as standalone answers, schema review (FAQPage, HowTo, Product), distribute on G2/Reddit/YouTube.
   - 90 days: net-new content built AEO-first, internal search-intent taxonomy, attribution model that includes assisted/branded touchpoints.
5. **Measurement when sessions lie** — a stack of KPIs: citation share of voice, brand mentions in AI responses, branded search volume lift, direct + branded organic, demo requests with "saw it on ChatGPT" attribution, share of pipeline from non-attributed channels.
6. **Tooling shortlist and how to evaluate** — Ahrefs Brand Radar, Profound, Peec AI, Otterly, manual prompt panels; criteria: prompt sample size, model coverage, refresh cadence, citation extraction accuracy, competitor benchmarking.
7. **Mistakes that waste a quarter** — chasing zero-volume "AI keywords," over-indexing on schema, blocking all LLM crawlers reflexively, treating AEO as a separate content track instead of a retrofit, ignoring third-party surfaces (G2, Reddit, YouTube transcripts).
8. **Recommended next action** — run a 1-week AEO baseline: 25 prompt panel × 4 models, audit top-20 pages, decide on crawler policy, present a single-slide measurement framework to the CMO.

### differentiation angle
Most AEO articles are generic, e-commerce-flavored, or vendor-pitch disguised as advice. This piece wins by:
- **B2B SaaS specificity**: pipeline language, comparison/alternatives pages, G2/Reddit citation surfaces, account-based measurement.
- **Retrofit-first stance**: assume the reader has 200+ existing articles and can't start over. Lead with what to change, not what to write.
- **Honest measurement**: name the KPIs that don't work (sessions, AI-keyword volume) and propose a defensible alternative the reader can actually present internally.
- **Tooling triangulation**: compare 3–5 named tools by methodology, not feature lists, and show when a manual prompt panel beats a vendor.
- **Concrete artifacts**: include a sample prompt panel, a before/after page rewrite, and a single-slide CMO framework — not just principles.
